Executive Overview

This interactive application outlines Ollion's comprehensive deployment strategy for DST's multi-cloud transformation. It expands on the high-level proposal, providing a detailed roadmap for implementing IaaS, Migration Services, Data Storage, and DRaaS. The approach focuses on a phased journey, starting with DST's UNN infrastructure and transitioning to white-labeled offshore CSP environments (AWS, GCP, Azure), delivering incremental value at each stage.

The core goal is to empower DST with enhanced agility, operational efficiency, and robust business resilience through a modern, multi-cloud architecture managed seamlessly by Ollion.

Purpose of This Application

To provide DST with a clear, interactive, and easily digestible overview of the detailed deployment plan. This application aims to instill confidence and clarity regarding the phased approach, key deliverables, and strategic value of the multi-cloud transformation initiative.

Strategic Imperatives

  • Enhance agility and operational efficiency.
  • Enable innovation and future growth.
  • Optimize costs through strategic cloud adoption.
  • Ensure robust business resilience and disaster recovery.
  • Deliver incremental and measurable business value.

Foundational Landing Zone Deployment

A landing zone (LZ) is a pre-configured, secure, and scalable environment that forms the cornerstone of cloud adoption. It enforces security, ensures compliance, drives operational efficiency, and accelerates workload deployment. This project starts with an UNN-based LZ, then transitions to white-labeled CSP LZs.

Initial UNN-Based Landing Zone Architecture

The initial private cloud LZ on UNN will focus on core IaaS, IAM, security, and operations, designed to support initial use cases and facilitate a smooth transition to CSPs.

  • Core Infrastructure (IaaS on UNN): Compute (VMs), segmented Network, on-premise Storage.
  • Identity and Access Management (IAM): Authentication, Authorization, Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), integration with existing DST identity systems.
  • Security and Governance: Security policies, perimeter/internal security, compliance controls, logging/monitoring.
  • Operational Model: Standardized provisioning, backup/recovery, change/configuration management.

Transition Pathway to White-Labeled CSP Landing Zones

Strategic transition to Ollion's white-labeled CSP LZs (AWS, Azure, GCP) aims to leverage CSP-specific strengths, enhance scalability, access advanced services, ensure global reach, and optimize costs. Ollion will manage these, abstracting complexity from DST.

CSP Landing Zone Design Principles:

  • Adherence to Well-Architected Frameworks (AWS, Azure).
  • Multi-Account/Subscription Strategy for isolation and governance.
  • Distinction between Platform Landing Zones (PLZs) and Application Landing Zones (ALZs).
  • Infrastructure as Code (IaC) for consistent, repeatable deployments.

Transition Flow: UNN to CSP

  1. Establish & Validate UNN Landing Zone & Pilot Workloads.
  2. Design & Build White-Labeled CSP Platform Landing Zone (e.g., AWS).
  3. Migrate/Rehost Pilot Workloads from UNN to CSP PLZ.
  4. Implement Ollion's White-Label Service Management for CSP.
  5. Expand to other CSPs (Azure, GCP) based on strategic fit for subsequent use cases.

Multi-Cloud Integration & White-Labeled Service Delivery

Ollion's strategy involves strategic CSP selection (AWS, Azure, GCP) based on use case needs, all delivered through a unified, white-labeled service model. This simplifies complexity, ensures consistent governance, and optimizes performance and cost for DST.

Ollion's White-Labeling Operating Model

  • Service Abstraction & Presentation: Unified Ollion service portal, standardized service catalog, Ollion branding for cohesive experience.
  • Billing Management & Cost Optimization: Consolidated invoicing, cost allocation/showback, proactive cost optimization by Ollion.
  • Support & Escalation: Single point of contact (Ollion L1/L2 support), defined SLAs, managed escalation to CSPs (L3).
  • Service Management & Governance: ITIL-aligned processes, policy enforcement, change management, performance/compliance reporting.

Cross-Cloud Governance, Security & Compliance

A unified framework across UNN and CSPs covering resource organization, identity management (federated/centralized), consistent security posture (CSPM tools), and compliance mapping/reporting.

Network Integration & Interoperability

Secure connectivity between UNN and CSPs (VPNs, Direct Connect/ExpressRoute), inter-CSP connectivity if needed, unified DNS strategy, IPAM, and consistent network security controls, managed by Ollion.

Risk Management:

Continuous risk identification, assessment, mitigation strategies, contingency plans, and a formal Risk Register.

Change Management:

Formal process for change request submission, impact assessment, approval, implementation, and communication.

Quality Assurance & Operational Readiness Reviews (ORR):

QA plan with quality standards, review cycles, and comprehensive testing. ORRs before major service go-lives ensure all technical, operational, and support aspects are ready.

Key Considerations and Recommendations

Proactive strategic considerations to ensure DST's long-term success and value realization from this multi-cloud transformation.

Identification, assessment, and strategic remediation of existing technical debt during migration and modernization. Preventing new debt through cloud-native design principles and IaC. DST should allocate resources for ongoing management.

Skills assessment for DST teams. Ollion to provide/facilitate tailored training on the white-labeled service, cloud consumption, specific technologies, and cloud financial management. Encourage continuous learning.

Ollion as the single point of contact, managing underlying CSP relationships, performance, and SLAs on behalf of DST. DST should establish clear review processes with Ollion.

Continuous security monitoring, joint incident response plan, ongoing compliance management (audits, reporting), security updates and patching managed by Ollion. DST active participation in reviews is key.

Architecture designed for scalability. Ollion to work with DST on evolving the service catalog, incorporating new CSP services, and advising on new technologies (AI/ML, serverless) through regular strategic reviews.
